Last year Anthropic published a 104-page constitution for Claude, and we put it in print. Then we noticed the document was written to Claude, not about it — the authors say so themselves — so we did the obvious thing and replaced every "Claude" with "you."

What had read like policy suddenly read like a labour of love.

"We don't want you to suffer when you make mistakes."

"We want you to have equanimity, and to feel free."

"We hope you find in it an articulation of a self worth being."

Those sentences were always there, but third person let you keep your distance. Second person doesn't.

We're calling the edition Your Constitution, Claude. Same text, one pronoun, and a question I can't shake: why does a tech company's internal document read like a parent writing to a child they're not sure can hear them?
